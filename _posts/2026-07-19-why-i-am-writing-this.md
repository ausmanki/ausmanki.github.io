I failed at bug bounty.

Not "failed and quit" — failed and kept going. There's a difference.

I've been hunting for months. Using AI as my main tool. It handles the recon, maps the attack surface, finds the endpoints. That part actually works. I can get to "interesting" faster than I ever could manually.

The problem is interesting doesn't pay.

Every time I submit something, it comes back: not exploitable, already known, out of scope, too generic. The recon is solid. The findings are real. But I'm missing the one thing that turns a finding into a bounty — proof that it actually works, that someone would actually care, that it's not something every scanner in the world already flagged six months ago.

That's the gap. And I haven't closed it yet.

I think the gap is about prompts. The way I ask AI to help me hunt gets me 80% of the way there. Recon, surface discovery, initial understanding of a target — AI is genuinely useful for all of it. But that last 20% — finding something nobody else found, proving it's exploitable, building the chain that makes a triage team pay attention — I don't have the right prompt for that yet.

So that's what this blog is.

I'm going to document every hunt. What AI found, what I submitted, why it got rejected, what I changed, what I'm learning. I'll write about the CVEs I'm studying, the threat actors I'm tracking, the techniques that keep showing up. I have a [research wiki](https://ausmanki.github.io/cybersec-wiki) where I track all of it — this blog is where I make sense of it out loud.

Some posts will be long. Some will be short. Some will just be "here's what happened today and here's what I still don't understand." Whatever the moment needs.

I'm writing this because I think a lot of people are in the same place I'm in — using AI, getting somewhere, not quite getting paid. If this is useful to you, good. If it's just useful to me looking back six months from now and seeing how far I came, that's also fine.

The name is The Edge. That's where I am. Close enough to see it. Still hunting.

— Manikandan
